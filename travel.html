Travel

We are so excited to have so many of you coming from out of town. We have tried to make things as easy as possible for you to figure out travel plans, what to do when you are here, etc.

Due to the remote and rural location of our wedding, you will most likely need to fly into an airport, rent a car, and drive the rest of the way to your accomodations in Nelson (or nearby). We will be creating a carpool list after invites are sent out to help facilitate splitting rental cars, for folks arriving and leaving at similar times. Stay tuned for that.

Airports

You have a few nearby airport options to choose from. West Kootenay Regional Airport (YCG) in Castlegar, BC will be the closest airport to Nelson, BC. You can rent a car from PractiCar, located right at the airport, and drive the remaining 30-40 minutes to arrive at Nelson, BC. Alternatively, you may choose to fly into Kelowna International Airport (YLW) in Kelowna, BC. Located approximately 4 hours from Nelson, YLW has Enterprise Rent-a-Car options for you to pick up a rental car.

If you fancy a longer road trip, you may consider flying into Calgary International Airport (YYC), located in the next province over in Calgary, Alberta. From here, you may rent a car from one of the many car rental companies, explore the exciting city of Calgary, AB, and then take a road trip through Canada’s famed Rocky Mountains. This road trip can include stops to Banff National Park, Jasper National Park (via the amazing Icefields Parkway), Kootenay National Park, Fairmont Hot Springs, among several other beautiful stops. We recommend taking a few days to do this if you want to experience the magic of the Rocky Mountains.

Finally, for a British Columbia roadtrip that is sure to impress, you may consider flying into Vancouver International Airport (YVR), located in Vancouver, BC. YVR has several options to rent a car directly from the airport, or you may choose to stay in Vancouver for a day or two to explore the sights of this beautiful coastal city. From here, you may then begin your road trip across British Columbia, which includes driving along the Fraser Valley, the Okanagan Valley (if you are a fan of wine, you’ll love this area), and the (almost) desert region of Osoyoos. This is the road trip Allie and her family have done every year since she was a child, and it still has not gotten old!

Accomodations

We would suggest at the very least staying in town the night of the wedding (i.e., the night of September 7th), but there are several other lovely accomodations nearby if you choose to stay a while. You should consider booking sooner rather than later if you know you are going to be able to make it.
